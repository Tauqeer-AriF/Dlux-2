About DJ DLUX

The Architect
of the UK Sound

"I don't play one genre,
I play the culture."

Since 1986, DJ Dlux has been a pillar of British underground music — a true pioneer of the Pirate Radio movement whose career spans over four decades of cultural evolution. He isn't merely a witness to the history of the UK sound; he is one of its primary architects.

Joining the legendary Brain Records in 1991, he became a central figure in the development of Hardcore and Jungle, working alongside Bizzy B and producing with Lennie De Ice and DJ Hype. His work during this era established a technical standard and an authentic Jungle pressure that remains his most noted contribution to the culture.

Career Timeline

Four Decades of Legacy

1986
The Beginning

Entering the Underground

DJ Dlux emerged from the UK underground scene, embracing the pirate radio movement that was reshaping British culture. A young selector with a passion for sound and community, he began building the foundation for what would become a legendary career.

1991
The Foundation

Brain Records · Jungle & Hardcore Pioneer

Joining the legendary Brain Records, Dlux became a central figure in the development of Hardcore and Jungle. Working alongside Bizzy B, with productions featuring Lennie De Ice and DJ Hype, he helped forge the pioneering sounds that defined the early 90s UK rave scene.

2011
Innovation

We Play Music — Live from London

As the longest-serving member and Head of Deja Vu FM, Dlux created We Play Music — an original concept combining live bands and artist performances, broadcast live from venues across London. A bridge between underground radio and live music culture.

2021
The Mission

Creative Engagement Services

Drawing on his experience as a specialist coordinator in conflict mediation and youth exploitation for local government, Dlux founded Creative Engagement Services — a platform utilising deep knowledge of the creative industries to empower the next generation and provide genuine support for young people.
